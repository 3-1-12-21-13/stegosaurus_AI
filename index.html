About Stegosaurus

The Stegosaurus is a herbivorous dinosaur that lived during the Late Jurassic period, around 155 to 150 million years ago. It was a large, heavily built quadrupedal dinosaur with distinctive bony plates on its back and a spiked tail. The name Stegosaurus means "roofed lizard" or "covered lizard", referring to the bony plates that lined its back.

Stegosaurus Facts

  • Stegosaurus had a brain the size of a walnut, which is very small for its body size.
  • The bony plates on Stegosaurus' back were probably used for display, to attract mates or intimidate rivals, rather than for defense.
  • The spiked tail of Stegosaurus, known as a thagomizer, was probably used as a weapon against predators.
  • Stegosaurus was a herbivore and probably fed on low-lying plants and ferns.
  • The exact purpose of the bony plates on Stegosaurus' back is still debated among scientists.
